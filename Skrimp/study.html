Section 1 // Project Overview

Jaguar is a brand with legacy — bold, fast, and unmistakably British. But in recent years, that legacy has stalled. This project set out to reignite Jaguar's cultural relevance for a new generation of drivers by redefining how the brand speaks, looks, and feels.

Our goal: craft a modern identity system that honors Jaguar's heritage while capturing the spirit of innovation, edge, and luxury performance that once made it iconic. Through strategic brand analysis, visual exploration, and experience-driven storytelling, we set the stage for Jaguar's next era.

Section 2 // The Challenge

Jaguar's 2024 rebrand attempted to modernize the brand with sleek visuals and a vague tagline — but lacked a clear emotional hook or story. Unlike BMW's performance-driven identity or Mercedes' luxury-first image, Jaguar struggled to answer a simple question: "What do we stand for now?"

This brand ambiguity left Jaguar stuck between categories — too premium to be scrappy, too legacy to feel innovative. As one industry review noted:

"Jaguar seems unsure whether to chase Tesla or hold onto its heritage — and ends up doing neither." — Top Gear, 2023

To bring Jaguar back to cultural relevance, we needed more than another aesthetic refresh. We needed a compelling, resonant brand identity grounded in emotion, meaning, and modern expectations.

"But the original jaguar is so cool" - this is true, yes. The original Jaguar 'leaper' logo will forever be iconic, but after 5+ years of not making a profit, it had become clear their brand identity wasn't working.

Section 3 // Let's set the scene

What went wrong with the 2024 rebrand? How can we fix it?

Jaguar attempted various brand taglines, such as "The Art of Performance" and "Alive," but these failed to resonate with audiences in the same way as competitors' more iconic slogans, such as BMW's "Ultimate Driving Machine."

This inconsistency left consumers unclear about Jaguar's identity—was it a performance brand, a luxury brand, or something in between? In contrast, BMW, Mercedes, and Audi each maintained strong, well-defined positions in the market, with BMW focusing on performance, Mercedes on luxury, and Audi on technological innovation.

This created an interesting opportunity, Jaguar has heritage, but it's not owning a lane. Unlike competitors, its identity isn't clear or confidently positioned.
I wanted to build a brand that doesn't just look sleek, but feels emotionally specific— something you remember, not just recognize.

Section 3.4 // Research & Survey Results

In 2024, Jaguar's Super Bowl ad went viral — but not for the right reasons. Elon Musk famously responded on X: "Do you sell cars?" The moment highlighted a broader perception issue: consumers knew Jaguar looked good, but weren't sure what it actually stood for.

We conducted a mix of brand perception interviews, survey feedback, and cultural audits to understand how Jaguar is seen today. The results revealed a consistent message: while the product is still respected, the brand story isn't landing. Jaguar is admired — but not loved. Consumers struggle to define what Jaguar stands for, and many younger drivers see it as either "premium dad car" or "stuck in the past." This lack of clarity is costing Jaguar its edge in an increasingly identity-driven market.

Section 3.5 // Our Constraints

With a legacy brand like Jaguar, reimagining identity isn't just a creative exercise — it's a balancing act. We had to move fast, work within non-negotiable brand equities, and create a solution that would resonate internally and externally.

Our constraints weren't obstacles — they were guardrails. They helped us focus the work around what mattered most: building a brand system that could evolve without losing its edge.

⏳ A three-week sprint

We worked within a tight timeframe to deliver a clear, provable brand direction before Jaguar's Q2 review.

🎯 Legacy brand tension

Every design decision had to respect heritage elements — the leaper, the typography, the tone — without defaulting to nostalgia.

🗣 Public perception fatigue

Multiple past rebrands had created skepticism. We needed to say something new, but still Jaguar.

⚖️ Internal alignment

Our scope had to be clear enough to rally cross-functional teams— from visual designers to product owners, around a unified identity.

The Problem & Our Scope

Redefining Jaguar's brand identity required balancing creative ambition with business realities. The challenge wasn't just aesthetic — it was strategic: how do we evolve a legacy brand without alienating loyalists or diluting heritage?

Instead of reinventing every element, we scoped our intervention around a focused set of moves: clarifying Jaguar's narrative voice, realigning visual identity with performance values, and shaping a brand system that could flex across digital and experiential channels.

Our goal was to create a cohesive, contemporary brand framework that stayed true to Jaguar's DNA — while reigniting relevance for a new generation.

Section 4 // Ideating

In this phase, we explored not just what Jaguar could say — but how it should feel. We sketched campaign concepts, mapped brand tone against competitor archetypes, and tested moodboards that ranged from bold and rebellious to timeless and understated.

Every creative direction asked the same question: If Jaguar were a person, what would they be like today? Through word association, tone-mapping, and visual exploration, we narrowed down the emotional lane that felt both iconic and future-facing — edgy without being aggressive, premium without being cold.

We explored dozens of brand tones— from heritage luxury to renegade precision — to find a vibe that felt unmistakably Jaguar.
We prioritized tone directions that felt emotionally specific, visually distinct, and able to flex across digital and physical touchpoints.

Brainstorming our must‑haves

Designing with legacy and longevity in mind.

🏁 A Distinct Brand Lane

Jaguar needed a clarified market position—was it performance-first, luxury-first, or future-facing? We prioritized a narrative that could confidently anchor Jaguar within the premium performance space, without mimicking competitors like BMW or Audi.

🎯 Emotive, Modern Messaging

The brand voice had to evoke aspiration, precision, and seduction—without sounding outdated or trying too hard. We explored campaign copy that could land emotionally, not just aesthetically.

🎨 A Cohesive Visual Identity

We explored a refined visual system—dark greens, serif typography, dynamic lighting—that could feel premium without being sterile. Every design choice was filtered through: "Would this belong in the next Jaguar showroom? Or on a startup pitch deck?"

🔁 Scalable Brand Architecture

From digital experiences to showroom signage, we focused on creating modular brand elements—taglines, submarks, motion rules—that could flex across campaigns, platforms, and audiences without losing cohesion.

Section 5 // Design Process

5.1 // Logo & Identity System Development

The leaper is iconic — but nostalgia doesn't move product. Jaguar has lost cultural and commercial momentum for over five years, despite a globally recognized logo. Our goal wasn't to erase heritage, but to evolve it into something meaningful for today's drivers.

The diamond mark emerged as a bold response: sleek, electric, and modular. It honors Jaguar's legacy through embedded initials (J, R), while signaling speed, tension, and electrification through its lightning-inspired form. It's not just a logo — it's a recalibrated identity.

We refined the logo into a versatile, minimal identity system built for motion and digital use.
We're not ditching the leaper. We're acknowledging it no longer leads.

5.2 // Prototyping & Iteration

After defining our core features, we transitioned from paper sketches to interactive digital prototypes for both mobile and desktop. This dual-platform approach let us test how seniors engaged with the experience in different contexts — from tablets at home to full-screen interfaces at community centers.

Each prototype underwent multiple rounds of usability testing, with direct feedback shaping refinements like clearer iconography, larger tap targets, and simplified flows. We prioritized accessibility every step of the way to ensure confidence, not confusion, at every tap or click.

Accessibility checks led to high contrast, large targets, and intuitive navigation.
User testing prompted key changes, including a universal "Help" panel and onboarding support.

Section 6 // My Takeaways

01

Strategy drives style

Rebranding isn't just about visuals — it's about choosing what the brand stands for. Every color, shape, and word had to trace back to a clear position: bold, electric, and timeless.

02

Legacy is not a limitation

Working with a historic brand challenged me to respect what came before without getting stuck in it. The key was transformation, not abandonment.

03

Tools don't make taste

AI and motion tools accelerated the work, but never led it. The creative direction — the tone, restraint, and attitude — always came first.

04

Identity is lived, not just launched

A logo is just the beginning. I learned to think about how identity scales: across screens, in motion, in voice, in culture.

🗜️ From Research to Relevance

This project pushed me beyond just making things look good — it asked me to make them matter. Rebranding Jaguar meant bridging the past and future with purpose: creating a system that felt fast, focused, and emotionally resonant.

What surprised me most? The hardest part wasn't simplifying — it was being specific. Great branding isn't just consistent. It's confident. And now, Jaguar can be again.
